Colors

The color theme for Atomzier focuses on the details and uses color to express emotion, action, and prominence.

The color palette is comprised of nine core colors. The greys are used for backgrounds, borders and elements that are disabled. The brightest colors in the palette - red, yellow and green - are used to draw attention to a user. They maybe used for alerts, actions that cannot be undone, or descriptive labels that need extra attention. Finally, the most dominate colors - black and blue - are used for navigation and basic interaction tasks.

When adding new components or features, consider the usage first and then assign colors. By standardizing the palette and reserving certain colors for particular actions, the overall experience will benefit.

Typography

Atomizer leverages one typeface and two weights for its interface. Proxima Nova, designed by Mark Simonson Studio, gives the interface a professional look with just enough personality.

The dominate weight used is regular. This style of the font should be used in almost all cases. When a text element needs additional attention the bold weight can then be used. You will notice that the bold weight is used primarly for actions, labels, and areas where strong emphasis is needed. Iconography

Basic shapes mixed with straight lines define the iconography set for Atomizer. The simple icons are used to draw attention to items users can take action on.

Icons should be used sparingly throughout the application's interface. When text takes up too much room or an action needs special attention an icon may be used. Below is a sample of the icons avaliable to use. Icons will be added to the kit as the interface grows.
